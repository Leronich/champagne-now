Vintage Bollinger and Aged Comté: A Perfect Champagne Cheese Pairing

Thirty-six month aged Comté from Jura, its paste the color of amber honey, cut into generous triangles. The cheese offers layers of hazelnut, brown butter, and mineral notes from Alpine pastures, with crystalline pockets that crunch pleasantly between teeth.

The Pour
Vintage Champagne Bollinger La Grande Année 2014 $95

Vintage Bollinger's extended lees aging creates the textural weight and flavor complexity needed to stand alongside aged Comté's intensity. The wine's characteristic biscuity depth and pronounced structure complement rather than compete with the cheese's nutty richness.

Both products share patient maturation as their defining characteristic. Comté develops its crystalline texture and concentrated flavors through careful aging in Jura's mountain caves, while Bollinger's vintage releases benefit from extended time on lees in Ay's crayères. The wine's brioche-like complexity and mineral backbone echo the cheese's nutty depth and terroir-driven character. Each sip and bite reveals new layers — the Champagne's fine persistent mousse cleansing the palate between encounters with Comté's rich, almost caramelized notes.

The Ritual

Bring the Comté to room temperature an hour before serving. Pour the Bollinger into flutes chilled in the refrigerator, not freezer. Cut the cheese into thumb-sized wedges, ensuring some pieces include those prized crystalline pockets. Take a small piece first, chew slowly to release the nutty oils, then sip the Champagne while the cheese flavors linger. The wine's acidity will refresh your palate while its complexity harmonizes with the cheese's finish.

Also Works With
24-month aged Gruyère

If Comté proves elusive, well-aged Gruyère offers similar nutty complexity and crystalline texture, though with slightly more pronounced fruit notes that complement Bollinger's apple and pear undertones.

✦

In Champagne houses, we often serve aged Comté during harvest tastings — its richness provides necessary sustenance during long days in the cellar, while its complexity doesn't overwhelm developing vintage wines.

The Right Glass

Traditional Girolle Cheese Scraper — Creates delicate cheese rosettes that melt more readily on the tongue, allowing better integration with the Champagne's bubbles and preventing the dense cheese from overwhelming the wine's finesse.
