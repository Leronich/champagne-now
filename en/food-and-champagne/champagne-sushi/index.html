Blanc de Blancs and Sushi: When Minerality Meets Umami

Fresh sushi — particularly nigiri with clean-fleshed fish like yellowtail, sea bass, or tuna. The rice still warm, fish at perfect temperature, wasabi applied with restraint. No heavy sauces, no tempura complications. Just fish, rice, and the chef's precision.

The Pour
Blanc de Blancs Billecart-Salmon Blanc de Blancs $75

Pure Chardonnay brings the mineral backbone that echoes the ocean without competing with it. The tight acidity cuts through the rice's subtle richness while the wine's salinity harmonizes with the fish. Chablis-like precision meets Japanese restraint.

Sushi is architecture — each element balanced against the others. The rice provides gentle starch, the fish offers clean protein and oceanic minerals, the wasabi adds heat without masking. Blanc de Blancs respects this precision. Its mineral thread connects with the sea, its acidity refreshes between bites, its bubbles cleanse without overwhelming the delicate fish oils.

The Ritual

Serve the Champagne at 8°C in tulip glasses. Take a sip first, alone, to establish the wine's mineral character. Then taste the sushi — yellowtail nigiri works perfectly — and notice how the Champagne's salinity amplifies the fish's natural sweetness. The rice becomes more prominent, the wasabi more integrated. Each subsequent piece reveals new harmonies.

Also Works With
Sashimi with ponzu

When the citrus in ponzu meets Champagne's acidity, they create a bright duet that makes the fish taste even more like the ocean itself.

✦

I once served this pairing to a sushi master from Kyoto. He paused, smiled slightly, and said the wine 'understood the fish.' High praise from someone who speaks primarily through his knives.

The Right Glass

Japanese sake cups (ochoko) — Their small size echoes the portion control of sushi while the ceramic stays cool to the touch, keeping both wine and palate refreshed.
